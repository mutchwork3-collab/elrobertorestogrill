El Roberto Restaurant Grill started as a small family gathering around a backyard pit. Today, it stands as a testament to the power of honest ingredients and the perfect temperature.

Every cut of meat is hand-selected and seasoned with our signature blend before meeting the intense heat of local charcoal. It's not just a meal; it's the culmination of Roberto's lifelong obsession with the perfect char.

Our signature selections for the true meat enthusiast

Lugaw - Unli

₱50.00

Lugaw is best translated in English as Filipino rice porridge or rice gruel. It is a savory, thick comfort food made by boiling glutinous or regular rice with water, ginger, and garlic until the grains are soft, often served with toppings like toasted garlic, green onions, egg and chicken.

Chicken Inasal - Unli Rice

₱150.00

It is made from chicken, typically served by the breast and wing (Pecho) or leg and/or thigh (Paa/Hita) parts, that is marinated in a mixture of calamansi, black pepper, coconut vinegar and annatto before being grilled over hot coals while basted with the marinade.

Grilled Liempo

₱190.00

Inihaw na Liempo is Filipino grilled pork belly marinated in soy sauce and citrus, then cooked over charcoal. “Inihaw” means grilled. “Liempo” is pork belly. Filipino marinades for grilled pork typically use soy sauce, calamansi, lime, or lemon, and garlic.

Lomi - Solo

₱100.00

"Lomi" primarily refers to a thick Filipino egg noodle soup originating from Batangas, served hot with pork, liver, and seafood. It also refers to the Hawaiian word for "massage" or "knead" (used in lomi-lomi massage and lomi salmon). Additionally, it is a brand of home kitchen composter.
